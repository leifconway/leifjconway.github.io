Who am I?

My name is Eoin, pronounced Owen. I come from an Irish family, which is where the spelling comes from. I grew up in the suburbs of Chicago, went to private school my entire life. While I appreciate my education, there's no doubt in my mind that it sheltered me from a lot of the outside world. It wasn't until I got to college when I was exposed to so many new ways of thinking. I quickly became skeptical of everything I was previously taught. While I was raised Catholic, I now consider myself Agnostic, but I'm not even sure about that. This same skepticism has led me to many new social beliefs. The society that I grew up on was extremely superficial, and since then I've definitely broadened my horizons. Realizing that the way I lived was so mundane, I have become extremely curious about the the world around me and the lives that are lived within it.

Other than that, I consider myself to be a pretty reserved person. I tend to keep only a few close friends who are like family to me. I don't know what I'd do without them. I keep myself active with running daily, and skiing in the winter. I'd say my absolute favorite hobby is photography. I take my camera with me wherever I go. I rarely ever photograph myself, usually sticking to my surrounding or the people that I'm with.

What's my story?

So what brought me to where I am today? Why do I travel the world alone, making only temporary relationships? I was mid-way through my senior year studying marketing at The University of Minnesota - Twin Cities. I had a great group of friends, and I was honestly enjoying myself and my college experience very much. As I came to the mid-way point of my senior year reality hit me like a train. I realized I only had 4 months left before I entered the "real world". I was always told my by superiors to do the things that I want to do while I can, but I had yet to do any of those things. With only 4 months and so much to do, I put my education on pause. I decided to un-enroll in my classes and hit the road - literally. I packed up my hiking backpack, put things in the back of my truck and headed west. Just me and my camera, I've tried to document a journey. I say a journey rather than my journey because this story isn't about me, but rather the possibilities that anyone can take advantage of.

That was a year ago. I spent 3 weeks roadtripping around the united states. First across the midwest, then down the west coast. I documented the whole journey with my laptop and my camera.

My Goal

It's simple: to inspire people to beyond the bubbles that they live in.

The best way to learn is through experience and put push yourself outside of your comfort zone. I have found that people who have had the most experiences, met the widest variety of people, and who have seen the most places to be the most inspirational.
